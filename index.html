The creation of Hollywood Undead

June 3, 2005: The American rock rap band from Los Angeles, California, Hollywood Undead formed

2007: Phillips later left the group because of conflict with Erlichman.

September 2, 2008: They released their debute album, Swan Songs

November 10,2009: Released their live CD/DVD Desperate Measures

2010: The band annouced that singer, Deuce, hade left the band due to creative differences

2010: The bands long-time friend Daniel "Danny" Murillo joins the band to fill in Deuce's role

April 5, 2011: Released their second album, American Tragedy

July 13, 2013: Notes From The Undeground, album was released

March 31, 2015: Day of the Dead album was released

October 10, 2017: Founding member Da Kurlzz "amicably" parted ways with the group to "pursue his own interests"

October 27, 2017: They released their fifth album called Five (stylized as V)

Early 2019: During an interview with DEAD PRESS! they shared their plans to recored and released their sixth album

Current Members

Jorel "J-Dog" Decker – rap vocals, guitar, bass guitar, keyboards, synthesizer, programming (2005–present).

Dylan "Funny Man"  Alvarez – rap vocals (2005–present).

George "Johnny 3 Tears"  Ragan – rap vocals, screamed vocals, backing vocals (2005–present), bass guitar (2013–present).

Jordon "Charlie Scene"  Terrell – rap vocals, sung vocals, guitar (2005–present).

Daniel "Danny"  Murillo – sung vocals (2009–present), keyboards (2011–present), guitar (2013–present), bass guitar (2018–present).

Former Members

Jeffrey "Shady Jeff"  Phillips – rap vocals, synthesizer, samples (2005–2007).

Aron "Deuce"  Erlichman – sung vocals, rap vocals, screamed vocals, bass guitar, keyboards (2005–2009).

Matthew "Da Kurlzz"  Busek - screamed vocals, rap vocals, drums, percussion (2005–2017).
